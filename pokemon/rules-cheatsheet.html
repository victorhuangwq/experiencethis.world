Game Setup

  • Shuffle your 40-card deck 🔀
  • Draw 7 cards for your starting hand
  • Place 4 Prize cards face down
  • Place 1 Basic Pokemon as your Active Pokemon ⭐
  • Place up to 5 Basic Pokemon on your Bench (optional)
  • If you don't have any Basic Pokemon in hand:
    • Reveal your hand
    • Shuffle and draw 7 new cards
    • Opponent draws +1 card per mulligan

How to Win

  • Collect all 4 of your Prize cards 🏆
  • Knock out all of your opponent's Pokemon in play
  • If your opponent cannot draw a card when required

Your Turn

  1. Draw a card (except first player's first turn)
  2. You may do any of these in any order:
    • Put Basic Pokemon on your Bench (max 5)
    • Evolve your Pokemon
    • Attach 1 Energy card ⚡
    • Play Trainer cards
    • Retreat your Active Pokemon
    • Use Abilities
  3. Attack with your Active Pokemon (ends your turn) 🎯

First Turn Rules

  • First player does not draw a card ❌🃏
  • First player cannot attack 🚫🎯
  • First player cannot play Supporter cards 🚫📝
  • Both players can evolve Pokemon 🔄

Status Conditions

  • Asleep: Flip coin between turns. Heads = wake up
  • Confused: Flip coin to attack. Tails = 30 damage to self
  • Paralyzed: Cannot attack or retreat. Removed at end of turn
  • Poisoned: Place 1 damage counter between turns
  • Burned: Place 2 damage counters between turns, flip coin. Heads = remove condition

Additional Rules

  • You can have multiple of the same Basic Pokemon on your Bench 🪑
  • You can only attach one Energy card per turn ⚡
  • You can only play 1 supporter trainer card per turn 📝
  • When a Pokemon is knocked out, all cards attached to it go to the discard pile 🗑️
  • If your Active Pokemon is knocked out, you must replace it with a Benched Pokemon 🔄

Time Limit Rules

When time is called in a tournament match:

  1. Complete the current turn
  2. Play three additional turns ("+3 turns")
  3. If no winner is determined through normal win conditions, use the tiebreaker:

Tiebreaker Rules

  • Compare remaining Prize cards for both players
  • Player with fewer Prize cards remaining wins
  • If both players have the same number of Prize cards, the match is a tie
Note: This procedure follows official Pokemon TCG tournament rules for time limit situations.
